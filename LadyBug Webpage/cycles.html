Ladybugs

The Ladybug Life Cycle

Ladybugs go through four stages in their life cycle:

  1. Eggs: They start as yellow, oblong-shaped eggs.
  2. Larva: The eggs hatch into larvae, which have long, soft gray bodies but they haven't developed their wings yet.
  3. Pupa: Through eating and shedding their outer layer of skin several times, the larvae becomes a pupae.
  4. Adult: Finally, the pupae shed their last layer of skin and emerge as adult ladybugs. They start as yellow beetles and gradually turn into the color of their species, like red with two black spots, with the help of the sun and food.
