Ladybugs

A Fascinating World for Young Explorers

Hello, young explorers! Today, we're going on an exciting journey to learn all about ladybugs, those adorable little creatures that bring joy to our gardens and fields. Get ready to discover their secrets and amazing life cycle!

What are Ladybugs?

Ladybugs, also known as ladybirds or lady beetles, are not actually bugs, but beetles! They belong to the Coccinellidae family and are carnivorous, meaning they eat other insects.

Where Do Ladybugs Live?

The Two-Spotted Ladybug is found in the "Holarctic Realm", which includes North America, North Africa, the Indian subcontinent, the South Arabian Peninsula, and Eurasia (except for Southeast Asia). They can be found in forests, rivers, trees, shrubs, gardens, fields, and sometimes even in our houses!


How Many Types of Ladybugs Are There in Canada?

Did you know that there are over 553 different species of the common two-spotted ladybugs (the Adalia bipunctata which is there scientific name) around the world? In Canada, there are 59 species. Newfoundland has 23 different types. Even in St. John's, you can find 6 different species of ladybugs!

What Do Ladybugs Look Like?

Ladybugs have soft bodies protected by hard outer shells that cover both their bodies and wings. The most common ladybug is red with 2 black spots. But they can also be black, yellow ,brown, or orange. They can have anywhere from no spots up to 12 spots, or even bands and cross-hatched markings on their shells. As adults, they can grow up to 3.5 to 5.2 mm in length


What Do Ladybugs Do?

Ladybugs are nature's pest controllers! They eat insects that can destroy crops and plants in our gardens. Their favorite food is plant-eating insects like "APHIDS, which are insects like mealybugs and mites. Ladybugs have a huge appetite for these pests and help control infestations.

Are Ladybugs Harmful to Humans?

No, ladybugs are not harmful to humans. However, like many insects, they may emit a sticky, yellow, and stinky-smelling fluid as a defense mechanism when they feel threatened. This fluid can stain, but it's not dangerous.

Conclusion

Ladybugs are fascinating creatures that play an important role in our ecosystem. They are beautiful, helpful, and fun to learn about. Remember, if you ever see a ladybug, be gentle and respectful, and enjoy the wonder of nature!
